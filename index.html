Islam

Islam (Arabic: الإسلام; Alʾislām, "submission") is an
Abrahamic religion based on the dogma of absolute monotheism (تَوْحيد, tawheed)
and has its source in the Qur'an, considered to be the receptacle of
the word of God (الله, Allah) revealed, in the seventh century in Arabia,
to Muhammad (محمّد, Muḥammad), proclaimed by the adherents of Islam as the last prophet of God.

The 5 Pillars of Islam

Shahada

("Statement of Faith"):
أَشْهَدُ أَنْ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا ٱللَّٰهُ وَأَشْهَدُ أَنَّ مُحَمَّدًا رَسُولُ ٱللَّٰهِ
[a.sh.ha.du an la i.la.ha il.la.ala.hu wa.ash.ha.du an.na mu.ham.ma.dan ra.su.lu.la.hi]

Salat

Canonical prayer (five times a day):
الصبح (Al-Sobh)
الظهر (Al-Dohr)
العصر (Al-Asr)
المغرب (Al-Maghreb)
العشاء (Al-Ichâa)

saoum

fasting during the month of Ramadan. For Muslims, this means abstaining from eating, drinking, sexual intercourse, smoking, and committing sins

Zakat

Legal alms to the needy if you are taxable: it consists of a compulsory levy of 2.5% from a tax threshold of 20 dinars (valued at 84 grams of 18-carat gold)

Hajj

Pilgrimage: it consists of going to Mecca at least once, if one has the material and physical means to do so.

The Holy Quran

The Quran holds a paramount significance in Islam, regarded by Muslims as the verbatim word of God revealed to Prophet Muhammad over a span of 23 years. Comprised of 114 chapters, known as Surahs, this sacred scripture addresses a multitude of topics including faith, morality, and jurisprudence. Muslims perceive the Quran as the final and ultimate revelation, surpassing all previous scriptures, and consider it a comprehensive guide to life encompassing monotheism, prophethood, morality, and societal equity.

The History of Islam

Islam, one of the world's major religions, originated in the 7th century CE in the Arabian Peninsula with the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad. Muhammad, born in Mecca in 570 CE, received divine revelations over 23 years, which were compiled into the Quran. His message of monotheism and social justice spread rapidly, leading to the establishment of the first Islamic state in Medina in 622 CE. Islam expanded rapidly across the Arabian Peninsula and beyond, shaping the cultural, political, and intellectual landscape of the world. Today, Islam is a global faith with over a billion followers.
